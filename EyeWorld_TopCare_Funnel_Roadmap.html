Decision Engine
Logic Rules & Decision Triggers
Every rule below is a decision you should NEVER have to think about from scratch. When you see the trigger — the action is automatic.
📊 Ad Performance Rules
Triggers based on Meta Ads Manager data — check every morning.
WHEN
Any ad set's CPL crosses its kill-switch threshold for 2 consecutive days
Pause the ad set immediately. Do not reduce budget — pausing is cleaner than budget cuts during bad performance.
Notify supervisor same day with: ad set name, current CPL, kill-switch threshold, days over threshold.
Do not reactivate until new creative AND new copy are both ready. Reactivating the same creative will reproduce the same result.
WHEN
Frequency on any ad set crosses 2.5×
Do not pause the ad set yet. Flag it for creative refresh within 5 days.
Brief Bakr + Asmaa that same day for 2 new creative variations — completely different visual approach.
If frequency hits 3.0× before new creative is ready → pause the ad set. Do not keep spending on a fatigued audience.
WHEN
An ad set's CPL drops below 70% of its target CPL (exceptional performance)
Scale budget by 30–50% in a single step. The algorithm is optimized — gradual scaling at this point is slower with no benefit.
Monitor for 48 hours after scale. If CPL stays stable or improves → scale again by 20%.
Never change the creative, audience, or targeting when a campaign is performing exceptionally. Only touch the budget.
Current example: Lasir at 1.12 EGP — scale now, touch nothing else.
WHEN
A new ad set has under 50 results and has been running less than 7 days
Do not optimize, pause, or change anything. The algorithm is in learning phase — any edit resets the learning.
Exception: if CPL is already more than 3× the kill-switch with 20+ results → pause. The signal is already strong enough to act.
Current example: محافظات campaigns (Giza, Fayom, Benha) — wait for 50 results each before any judgment.
WHEN
A/B test (two creatives in same ad set) has been running 3+ days with 20+ results each
Compare CPL of each creative. The one with lower CPL is the winner — even if the margin is small.
Pause the losing creative. Do not delete it — archive for reference.
Put 100% of budget behind the winning creative. Splitting budget between winner and loser reduces overall efficiency.
WHEN
All ads stop unexpectedly (payment error, account flag, policy violation)
Step 1: Check Meta billing page first — payment issue is the most common cause. Pay balance or update card.
Step 2: After payment clears, manually reactivate each paused ad set — they don't always auto-resume.
Step 3: Check account spending limit — reset if needed after billing fix.
Step 4: Notify supervisor with how many hours ads were stopped — recalculate the day's expected results vs actual to assess loss.
📱 Organic Content Rules — Tied to Live Ad Performance
What to post, when, and how it should react to what the paid campaigns are doing.
WHEN
A paid campaign is actively running and CPL is healthy (under target)
Post 2–3 organic content pieces this week that reinforce the same message as the running ad — this warms the retargeting pool organically.
Content type: educational, testimonial, or social proof — same service the ad is promoting.
Do NOT post a competing service heavily the same week. Focus organic on what paid is pushing.
WHEN
A paid campaign's CPL is rising (approaching kill-switch) but you don't have new creative ready yet
Increase organic content immediately — post more frequently (daily) on that service to maintain reach while paid performance weakens.
Use organic to buy time: more posts, more stories, more engagement = maintains audience warmth without spending on a fatigued ad.
Schedule a Live session for that service this week — live content generates the highest organic reach and can partially compensate for dropping ad performance.
ALWAYS
On the day a Live session is scheduled
Post minimum 3 story reminders: 9am, 2pm, 7pm (1 hour before).
Increase ad budget by 20% that day only — live drives page visits which helps Meta's algorithm deliver ads more efficiently.
After live ends: trim the best 2–3 minutes of the live and post it as a Facebook video post within 24 hours — give it a clear title and description. This extends the live's reach significantly to people who missed it.
Collect all questions asked during live → turn them into the next week's FAQ content.
ALWAYS
On Fridays and Saturdays (Egyptian weekend)
Do not launch new ad sets on Fridays. CPM is higher on weekends, learning phase is less efficient, and results are less predictable.
Post 1 Facebook video or strong image post on Friday — organic reach peaks on weekend. Let it work for free.
Story CTA is allowed — WhatsApp link reminder — but keep it soft, not hard-sell.
Saturday: 1 story only. Rest the account and audience. Over-posting on rest days reduces weekly average engagement rate.
WHEN
An ad's comment section has patient questions or concerns
Reply within 4 hours — unanswered comments on ads reduce social proof and hurt ad performance scores.
If the question is medical (dosage, recovery, side effects) → reply "تواصل معنا على الواتساب عشان نقدر نساعدك أكتر" and DO NOT answer the medical question publicly.
Positive comments: like and reply warmly. These increase ad social proof which Meta rewards with better delivery.
If multiple people ask the same question in comments → that question becomes this week's FAQ post topic.
📸 Story Posting Logic
When to post what type of story — based on where you are in the week and what the ads are doing.
ALWAYS
Post a minimum of 1 story every day — even on rest days
Accounts that post daily stories maintain higher page health scores and better ad delivery from Meta's algorithm.
Minimum story: behind the scenes, team moment, morning greeting, or a quote relevant to your service. 30 seconds or less.
Story type by day: Sun = educational, Mon = product/service, Tue = engagement (poll/Q&A sticker), Wed = Live promo (3 reminders: 9am/2pm/7pm), Thu = CTA/booking link, Fri = emotional/inspirational, Sat = team/behind scenes. Note: Facebook Stories are separate from feed posts — both can run the same day without competing.
WHEN
An ad has been running for 3+ days and results are coming in steadily
Push a story that mirrors the ad message — same offer, same service — to reinforce the message for people who saw the ad but didn't click yet.
Include the WhatsApp link as a swipe-up or link sticker in the story — same destination as the ad.
This creates a "surround sound" effect — patient sees the ad, then sees the story, conversion rate improves.
NEVER
Post more than 7 stories in a single day
Story fatigue is real — after 5–7 stories, audience drop-off rate increases sharply. You're hurting your own reach.
Ideal: 3–5 stories per day. Space them: morning (9–10am), midday (1–2pm), evening (7–9pm).
If you have more content to share → save it for the next day or turn it into a feed post instead.
ALWAYS
Facebook-only content format priorities (no Instagram, no TikTok)
Best performing Facebook formats in order: Facebook Live → Native Facebook Video → Photo Post → Carousel → Link Post. Text-only posts perform worst.
Facebook videos should be uploaded natively (not shared from YouTube) — native video gets 3× more reach than external links on Facebook's algorithm.
Optimal post times for Egyptian Facebook audience: 8–10am (morning scroll), 1–3pm (lunch break), 7–10pm (evening peak). Thursday and Friday evenings are highest reach windows of the week.
Facebook Live minimum duration: 20 minutes. Lives under 10 minutes do not get algorithmic boost. The longer the live (up to 60 min), the more Facebook promotes it organically.
Facebook story link sticker: always add WhatsApp number as a link sticker in booking-CTA stories — this is the only way to make Facebook stories clickable.
💰 Budget Decision Rules
When and how to move money between campaigns.
NEVER
Make more than one budget change to the same ad set in 24 hours
Each budget change partially resets the learning phase. Multiple changes in one day = full reset = wasted spend while the algorithm re-learns.
Make one change, then wait 24–48 hours before touching the budget again.
WHEN
Monthly budget is 70% spent and it's only the 20th of the month
Do not add more budget to poor performers. Shift remaining budget entirely to the best-performing ad sets.
Pause all ad sets with CPL above target for the last 3 days — concentrate remaining budget on proven winners only.
Flag to supervisor: "We're at 70% budget on day 20 — recommend pausing X, Y, Z to protect end-of-month results."
ALWAYS
Before launching any new campaign or ad set
Use the Target Advisor in the dashboard app first — get AI-suggested CPL target, kill-switch, and reach estimate before setting up in Meta.
Set the kill-switch value in the dashboard app BEFORE the campaign goes live — not after it starts overspending.
New campaigns need a minimum 7-day learning period and at least 500 EGP budget to generate reliable signal. Under-budgeted campaigns never properly optimize.
